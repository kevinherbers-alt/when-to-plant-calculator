About When To Plant Calculator

Garden timing questions all have real answers, but they're scattered across seed packets, zone charts, and extension office tables. When To Plant Calculator pulls that timing knowledge into three simple tools that work from the two dates every gardener can look up: their last spring frost and first fall frost.

The when-to-plant calculator handles spring: enter your last frost date and a crop, and get dates for starting seeds indoors and moving plants outside. Warm-season crops like tomatoes need a long indoor head start and can't go out until frost danger passes, while cool-season crops like peas prefer to be in the ground weeks before the last frost — the calculator knows which is which.

The sprout and harvest calculator answers the questions that come after planting: when should these seeds come up, and when will there be something to pick? It also corrects for a subtlety that trips up many gardeners — "days to maturity" on a seed packet counts from transplanting for some crops and from sowing for others.

The fall planting calculator works backwards from your first autumn frost to find the last safe planting date for a fall crop, including the slower growth of shortening days and the extra leeway that frost-hardy crops earn.

All timing is presented as ranges based on typical values for each crop, because varieties genuinely differ — a cherry tomato and a beefsteak can be a month apart. Your seed packet always has the final word for your specific variety; these tools give you the framework to plan around it.
