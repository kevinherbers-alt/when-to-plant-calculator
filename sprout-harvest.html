Why sprouting times vary

Germination speed depends mostly on soil temperature and moisture. The ranges this calculator shows assume reasonable spring conditions — seeds in cold soil can take significantly longer, and warm soil speeds things up. If you're past the top of your sprout window by more than a week, common culprits are soil that's too cold, seeds planted too deep, soil drying out between waterings, or old seed with reduced viability.

What "days to maturity" really means

Days to maturity on a seed packet measures from direct sowing for crops usually sown in place, but from transplanting for crops usually started indoors, like tomatoes. That's why this calculator asks whether you planted seeds or transplants — the same tomato variety hits harvest around 70 days from transplant, but closer to 120 from seed. Your harvest window also isn't a single date: most crops produce over a stretch of weeks once they start.
