How fall planting deadlines work

A fall crop needs enough time to mature before cold weather shuts it down. The deadline is your first frost date, minus the crop's days to maturity, minus a "fall factor" of about two weeks — because shortening days and cooling temperatures slow growth compared to the spring rates seed packets assume. Frost-hardy crops like kale, spinach, and carrots get more leeway since they keep growing (and often taste sweeter) through light frosts, while tender crops like beans must finish before frost arrives.

The best crops for fall gardens

Fast-maturing, frost-tolerant crops shine in autumn: radishes, arugula, lettuce, spinach, turnips, and bok choy can go in remarkably late. Brassicas like broccoli and kale are classic fall crops — many gardeners find them easier in fall than spring since they mature in cooling weather rather than a heat wave, with fewer pest problems. Root crops like carrots and beets can be left in the ground past frost and harvested as needed.
